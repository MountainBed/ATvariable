In a symmetrical distribution, the pattern of frequencies on the left and right side are approximately mirror images of each other. In a skewed distribution, the scores pile up on one side of the middle and are spread out on the other side. If the scores pile up on the left, the distribution is skewed to the right, and if the scores pile up on the right, the distribution is skewed to the left. A distribution is unimodal if there is one very high area. A distribution is bimodal if there are two fairly equal high points.</text>
